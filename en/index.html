Extreme weather is forcing cities to rebuild their defenses；climate adaptation has become a test of public governance

Heat, floods and drought are straining urban drainage, energy, health systems and neighborhood response. Cutting emissions remains essential, but as climate risk reaches the street, a city's ability to protect vulnerable residents becomes the clearest measure of competent government.
